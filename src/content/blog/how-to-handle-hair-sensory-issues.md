## Setting Up a Sensory-Friendly Environment

Hair sensory issues can turn an everyday grooming routine into a stressful experience for many individuals and families. At Rejuvenate You, we understand that what feels like a simple brushing or washing task to most can trigger real discomfort, resistance, and emotional strain for those with heightened sensory sensitivities. This is especially true when dealing with hair washing, brushing, or even the sensation of hair lightly touching the face.

Those affected by hair sensory sensitivities experience touch differently, often feeling ordinary grooming as painful rather than merely uncomfortable. Heightened scalp sensitivity, tactile defensiveness, aversion to sound, feelings of helplessness, and environmental triggers—like bright lighting or noisy tools—can all make hair care overwhelming. Many individuals, including children and adults, also associate these routines with anxiety tied to school, work, or social activities.

Hair sensory issues stem from much more than mere pickiness—they represent real differences in how the brain processes everyday sensations. Parents who grasp these underlying mechanisms gain valuable insights for handling daily hair care more successfully.

Sensory processing disorder (SPD) significantly alters how a child's nervous system handles ordinary information. For children with tactile defensiveness, the simple touch of a hairbrush against their scalp feels genuinely painful. Their brains mistakenly interpret these normal sensations as threats, triggering protective responses. This is particularly evident in cases of hypersensitive sensory processing disorder.

Tactile sensitivity stands as the primary culprit behind hair brushing difficulties. The physical contact of bristles against the scalp creates genuine discomfort rather than minor annoyance. Many children also experience actual pain or headaches from:

Bristles pulling through tangled hair

Hair accessories like clips and headbands pressing against the scalp

Elastic hair ties creating tension against sensitive skin

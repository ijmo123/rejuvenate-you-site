## Understanding Acne Scars and Why They Linger

Before we dive into solutions, it helps to understand what acne scars are and why they form. When your skin experiences inflammation from breakouts, the healing process can either leave behind **indentations or discoloration**—especially if your skin was repeatedly irritated, picked at, or inflamed over time.

### The Most Common Types of Acne Scars

At Rejuvenate You, we treat several types of acne scarring:

**Atrophic scars:** These are **indented scars** caused by tissue loss and are the most common type.
- **Ice pick scars** – narrow and deep
- **Boxcar scars** – wider with sharp edges  
- **Rolling scars** – soft depressions with sloping edges

**Hypertrophic or keloid scars:** Raised, thicker scars due to an **overproduction of collagen** during healing.

**Post-inflammatory hyperpigmentation (PIH):** Dark marks or spots that remain after acne has healed. While not true scars, they still affect tone and clarity.

<!-- IMAGE: Medical diagram showing different types of acne scars on face -->

### Why It Matters to Know Your Scar Type

Each scar type **responds best to specific treatments**. That's why accurate diagnosis—and a personalized plan—is key to real, lasting improvement.

### Microneedling for Indented Acne Scars

[Microneedling](/microneedling-scottsdale-az) is one of the **most effective ways to address atrophic scars**. Using ultra-fine needles, the device creates tiny channels in your skin, stimulating collagen and elastin production as it heals.

**Why Clients Love It:**

- Reduces the appearance of indented or textured scars
- Improves skin tone and elasticity
- Minimal recovery time (just redness for 24–48 hours)
- Natural, progressive results over a series of treatments

If your scars are deep or longstanding, microneedling may be the most powerful non-invasive option available.

### Dermaplaning for Surface Smoothness

If your concerns lean more toward **dullness, uneven tone, or leftover pigment**, dermaplaning can be a perfect first step. This gentle treatment removes the top layer of dead skin and peach fuzz, revealing a brighter, smoother canvas beneath.

**Ideal For:**

- Light post-acne pigmentation (PIH)
- Dry or flaky skin from past breakouts
- Clients who want an immediate glow without downtime

[Dermaplaning](/dermaplaning-scottsdale) also preps the skin beautifully for deeper treatments like chemical peels or microneedling—boosting their effectiveness.

### Chemical Peels for Renewed Radiance

**Chemical peels** use controlled exfoliation to slough off damaged skin layers and promote cell turnover.

**What They Help With:**

- Fading dark spots and acne-related discoloration
- Improving skin texture and tone
- Reducing shallow scars and stimulating collagen
- Smoothing roughness and boosting brightness

You may experience some flaking or redness for a few days post-treatment, but the results—more even, glowing skin—are worth it.

## At-Home Options: Supporting Your Skin Safely

While professional care offers the most transformative results, you can support your skin at home with the right habits and ingredients.

**Safe At-Home Treatments Include:**

- Gentle exfoliation using AHAs or enzyme cleansers
- Hydrating serums with hyaluronic acid to maintain skin barrier health
- Daily SPF to prevent PIH from getting worse
- Patience and consistency

**What to Avoid:**

- DIY chemical peels
- Abrasive scrubs
- Overuse of actives without guidance

If you've been consistent at home but haven't seen results—or your scars are deep or textured—**it's time to come in for professional help**.
